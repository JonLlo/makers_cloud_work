## Coach Guidance: Kata Sessions

**Purpose:** These katas serve as a bridge into week two. They are designed to keep everyone engaged while providing a structured environment for pairing. While not a critical curriculum requirement, they are excellent for reinforcing Python skills and logical problem-solving.

## Delivery

* **Format:** These as a "lab" or interactive workshop, with an intro, main and plenary.
* **Scheduling:** A single intensive session usually works best. Run it at the end of week 1 or start of week 2.
* **Setup:** The kata requirements are in kataN.py files
* **Solutions:** Use the `kataN_solution.py` files as a reference. Remind learners that these are "a" solution, not "the" solution, to encourage creative problem-solving.

### Intro

Give a brief explanation of what katas are, how the session will work and how the learners should get started.

### Main

Give the learners 30 mins to work on the katas. Doesn't matter how far they get but hopefully everyone will at least complete the first one. If not, we should rethink the katas and the timing.

> You can decide whether or not to pair people up

### Plenary

Regroup and ask learners to DM you (on Zoom) a one sentence update about how they got on. Invite some people to share their solutions. Try to get multiple solutions for each kata and ask the cohort to compare them. Use the solutions provided if you want.
